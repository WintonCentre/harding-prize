About the Prize

When we have a decision to make, we look for trustworthy evidence — yet balanced, useful information can be hard to find.

The Harding Prize celebrates communication that helps people understand evidence and make up their own minds.

  • ✔ Helps people make informed decisions
  • ✔ Presents balanced and transparent evidence
  • ✔ Avoids persuading the audience

What kind of evidence?

The Harding Prizes are for those who gather information that helps people make decisions without telling them what to do.

This could include decisions about health, finance, education, or major public issues like energy policy or taxation.

Subjects and formats

Entries may include articles, websites, reports, podcasts, social media posts, or other communications that can be consumed within ten minutes.

Communications must be in English and made public during the relevant year.

How is it judged?

  • ✔ Clear and balanced evidence
  • ✔ Transparency about uncertainty
  • ✔ Focus on helping the audience
